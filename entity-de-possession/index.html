What Is Entity De-Possession Therapy?

Entity De-Possession, or Spirit Releasement Therapy, is a clinical approach first described by Edith Fiore and developed further by Dr. William Baldwin. Under therapeutic hypnosis, safe communication is established with attached energies or discarnate entities. The goal is compassionate release — not confrontation or fear-based ritual.

At death, the human spirit usually passes to the Light. But sometimes discarnate spirits may not — due to shock, confusion, unresolved trauma, or intentional attachment. They may stay behind with the intention of attachment: concern for a relative, continuation of an addiction, unfinished business, or revenge. When they attach to a living person, they often choose someone displaying similar energy or vulnerability. The host may experience sudden personality changes, adopt the entity's emotions or addictions, hear voices, or suffer unexplained physical symptoms.

Vulnerability to attachment varies by individual circumstance. Illness, injury, drug use, emotional disturbance, trauma, or lowered spiritual/energetic boundaries may increase susceptibility. This is not about possession in the dramatic, frightening sense. It is about an energetic overlay that can be therapeutically released.

At MindAccess, entity de-possession is combined with past life regression therapy to address root causes and prevent re-attachment. During hypnosis, entities communicate through the host's voice, revealing their identity, circumstances of attachment, and reasons for staying. The therapeutic approach is compassionate — we do not fight or banish. Instead, we help the entity understand its situation and gently guide it to the Light, where it can continue its own spiritual journey. This frees both the client and the attached entity.

Capt. Avinash Kumar Verma brings special expertise in entity research and clinical spirit releasement, developed over 17+ years of intensive practice. His approach is evidence-based, thorough, and deeply compassionate — addressing not just the symptom of attachment but the underlying vulnerability and the spiritual well-being of all involved.
